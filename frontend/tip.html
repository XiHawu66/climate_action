TIPS

General Tips


  • Turn off lights and household appliances when they are not in use
  • Electrify your home by removing gas appliances
  • Look for energy star symbols when buying new products.




Lighting


  • Use IC rated LEDs, they allow you to safely install insulation directly above them.


Heating


  • Reverse cycle air conditioners are around three times as efficient as gas. While the most common heating in Melbourne is gas, most homes also have air conditioners. So there is often no additional installation costs to switch.
  • Set your thermostat to between 18 and 20 during winter when you need it. For each 1 degree increase your heating costs increase by 10-10%
  • Prevent heat loss by sealing cracks around windows, installing exhaust fan seals and seals under exterior doors.
  • Look for missing or misplaced insulation in the ceiling. Be sure to replace lights with AC rate LEDs before insulating over them.






Hot Water


  • Heating water up then cooling it again for use is inefficient. Turn your water heater down to 60°C. Although the temperature can fluctuate lower, it is recommended the system rise to 60°C once per day to prevent bacteria.
  • Install lower-flow shower heads and take shorter showers.
  • Consider upgrading to Solar or heat pump hot water. You may be eligible for Federal Small-scale Renewable Energy Credits of up to $950 in addition to Victorian Energy Upgrades scheme rebates of up to $1,000.






Refrigeration


  • 26% of Victorian homes have 2 or more fridges. These second fridges are often older, less efficient and placed in a hot location such as a garage. You can save around $100 per year by switching each excess fridge.



Cooking


  • Gas stoves don't use much energy and are often the last gas appliance to remove. However, removing the last gas appliance enables you to save around $300 per year in connection fees. There are also health concerns in burning gas inside the home. It is often compared to passive smoking.



Solar


When considering installing or upgrading solar. You may be eligible for :
  • Federal Small-scale Renewable Energy Credits. *
  • Federal Low interest loans announced in the 2023 budget. *
  • Victorian Solar Panel Rebate up to $1,400. *
  • Victorian Interest Free Loan up to $1,400. *
* Prices and government schemes accurate as at June 2023

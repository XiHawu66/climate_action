What happened to our earth

Fire

In a typical year, Australia emits around 104 million metric tons of carbon through the burning of fossil fuels. The fires in 2019 and 2020, plus the added effects of drought, added an extra 186 million tons on top of it.

Flood

The damage and impact of floods is varied, substantial and far reaching. The immediate impacts of flooding include loss of human life, damage to property, destruction of crops and loss of livestock. The ongoing emotional impact is substantial with increased emotional stress and physical illness from waterborne diseases.


link To An Article

Ozone Hole

Data collected in the upper atmosphere have shown that there has been a general thinning of the ozone layer over most of the globe. This includes a five to nine per cent depletion over Australia since the 1960s, which has increased the risk that Australians already face from over-exposure to UV radiation resulting from our outdoor lifestyle.
